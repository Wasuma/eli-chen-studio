The Therapist's Tech Stack for 2026

Four tools. Complete practice management. No bloat. | 7 min read

Why Most Therapists Have Too Many Tools

The average therapist in 2026 uses 8-12 different software products:

  • EHR (Epic, Athena, SimplePractice) — $200-500/month
  • Billing system — $100-300/month
  • Scheduling — $50-200/month
  • Video conferencing — $150/month
  • Backup storage — $50/month
  • Password manager — $30/month
  • Time tracking — $50/month
  • CRM or client database — $100/month

Total: $730-1,580/month, plus:

  • Learning time (each tool has a learning curve)
  • Context switching (your brain pays a cost every time you switch apps)
  • Data fragmentation (patient info spread across systems)
  • Integration gaps (tools that don't talk to each other)

There's a better way.

The Minimal Viable Stack

You need exactly four tools to run a modern therapy practice:

  1. Notion — Database (clients, notes, treatment plans)
  2. Google Calendar — Scheduling (free, syncs with everything)
  3. Zoom — Telehealth (video sessions, recordings)
  4. Stripe — Payment (billing and invoicing)

Everything else is optional. This stack costs under $150/month (including Zoom) and handles 95% of therapy practice needs.

Deep Dive: Each Tool & How to Use It

1. Notion (Database + Documentation)

Free or $10/month

What it does: Stores everything—clients, session notes, treatment plans, intake forms, billing records, compliance documentation.

Why it's the backbone:

  • One tool instead of separate EHR + CRM + note-taking
  • Fully customizable to your workflow (not locked into vendor's model)
  • Affordable ($0 for solo practice, $10/month for teams)
  • Your data stays yours (not locked in by a vendor)
  • HIPAA-safe with proper setup (encryption, access control)

What to build in Notion:

  • Client database with contact info, diagnoses, dates started/ended
  • Session database (date, duration, billable, linked notes)
  • Clinical notes database (SOAP format)
  • Treatment plans (goals, interventions, outcomes)
  • Intake form (Notion form people fill out before first session)
  • Invoice tracking (client, session count, amount owed, paid status)

Time to set up: 2-4 hours with a template. 1-2 weeks from scratch.

2. Google Calendar (Scheduling)

Free

What it does: Therapist availability, client appointments, session time blocking, availability links you share with clients.

Why not SimplePractice/Acuity Scheduling?

  • Google Calendar is free ($25/month saved)
  • Works with every video tool (Zoom, Google Meet, Jitsi)
  • Can embed in your website
  • Clients can add reminders to their own calendar

Setup:

  • Create your availability blocks (e.g., 9-12 MWF, 2-5 TTh)
  • Generate a shareable link via calendar.google.com/calendar/u/0/r/settings/shareable
  • Share with clients (they can book appointments directly)
  • Add Zoom meeting link to calendar event details
  • Notion can display your Google Calendar (built-in integration)

Alternative: If you want automated reminders and waitlist management, Calendly ($12/month) is the minimal paid option.

3. Zoom (Telehealth + Recording)

Free or $16/month (Pro)

What it does: Video sessions with clients, meeting recordings (for supervision/consultation), screen sharing, waiting room for security.

Free vs. Pro:

  • Free: 45-minute limit on group meetings (unlimited 1-on-1 sessions). Fine for solo practice until you hit scale.
  • Pro ($16/month): Unlimited meeting duration, cloud recording, better security.

HIPAA Compliance Tip: Enable Zoom's HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA). It's free and available when you request it. Use a waiting room, require password, disable recording if you prefer.

Setup:

  1. Create a Zoom account
  2. Request HIPAA BAA from Zoom support (free, takes 24 hours)
  3. Set default meeting settings: require password, use waiting room, disable participant recording
  4. Generate a personal meeting room link (same link every session)
  5. Add link to Google Calendar event and Notion client record

Alternative: Google Meet (free) works if you only do telehealth. Jitsi (free, self-hosted) is best if privacy is paramount.

4. Stripe (Payments + Invoicing)

2.2% + $0.30 per transaction

What it does: Charge clients, send invoices, track payments, integrate with insurance billing.

Why Stripe instead of PayPal?

  • Better for recurring clients (subscriptions, monthly billing)
  • Lower fees for therapists (2.2% vs PayPal's higher rates)
  • ACH transfer option (faster than wire transfer)
  • Integrates with Notion (automate invoicing)

Setup:

  1. Create Stripe account (identity verification required)
  2. Generate invoice links for clients who owe money
  3. Set up ACH direct deposit to your bank account (daily or weekly)
  4. Track payments in Notion (linked to client + session records)

Insurance Billing: If you bill insurance directly (not out-of-pocket), you may need additional tools (e.g., Athena, SimplePractice). Those typically cost $300-500/month but handle insurance-specific workflows.

The Complete Workflow

How these four tools work together in practice:

Client Onboarding:

  1. Send intake form (Notion form) → client fills it out
  2. Data auto-populates Notion client database
  3. Send Google Calendar link → client books first appointment
  4. Calendar event includes Zoom link

During Session:

  1. Join Zoom meeting (link from calendar)
  2. Take notes after session in Notion
  3. Link session to treatment plan goals

After Session:

  1. Log billable hours in Notion
  2. Client is invoice automatically (Stripe workflow or Notion form)
  3. Track payment status in Notion

Getting Started With This Stack

💡 Pro Tip: Set up in this order: (1) Notion database, (2) Google Calendar, (3) Zoom. Once those three are running, add Stripe for payments. Don't try to do everything at once.

Resources for setup:

  • Therapy practice management books on Amazon
  • Quality audio/video equipment for telehealth
  • Backup storage devices (paired with Google Drive for redundancy)

When to Upgrade From This Stack

This stack works for 90% of private practice therapists. Upgrade to EHR + dedicated PM software when:

  • You're managing 5+ therapists (need permission controls and shared access)
  • You bill insurance regularly (need automated claim submission)
  • You need to be in compliance with specific state/hospital requirements
  • You're opening multiple locations and need centralized management

For everyone else: this stack handles it better, costs less, and doesn't lock you into a vendor.
