The Notion SEO Setup: Core Databases

1. Master Client Database

Start with a single database listing every client:

  • Client name, contact info, billing address
  • Contract type (retainer, project, consulting)
  • Monthly rate and invoice date
  • Website URL and Ahrefs/SEMrush profile links
  • Key contacts (decision-maker, tech contact, finance)
  • Status: active, paused, archived

Pro tip: Add a formula field to auto-calculate days since last contact. Flag clients you haven't talked to in 30+ days for check-ins.

2. Keyword Tracking Database

This is your bread and butter. Create one entry per keyword:

  • Keyword term (e.g., "therapist scheduling software")
  • Search volume and keyword difficulty (from Ahrefs/SEMrush)
  • Current rank position (updates weekly)
  • Target position (your goal)
  • Client relationship (links to client database)
  • Status: ranking well, in progress, not ranked, competitor dominated
  • Historical rankings (track position changes month-to-month)
  • Page targeting (which URL is ranking for this keyword)

Add views: sort by "client," "keyword difficulty," "target position," or "status." Instantly see which keywords need attention.

3. SEO Audit Database

Log technical SEO findings:

  • Issue description (e.g., "Missing meta descriptions on product pages")
  • Severity: critical, high, medium, low
  • Pages affected (count or list)
  • Fix complexity: quick (<1 hour), medium (1-3 hours), complex (3+ hours)
  • Client relationship
  • Status: found, reported, in progress, fixed, verified
  • Notes on technical implementation

This becomes your audit report: filter by client, sort by severity. Email directly to clients.

4. Competitor Tracking Database

Monitor competitive landscape:

  • Competitor name and domain
  • Client they compete with
  • Their top 20 keywords (reference list)
  • Their Domain Authority (DA)
  • Backlink growth (month-to-month)
  • Content strategy notes
  • Gaps vs. your client (opportunities to exploit)

Use this for quarterly competitive analysis. Show clients where they can win.

5. Monthly Reporting Template

Auto-calculate KPIs for each client's report:

  • Total keywords tracked
  • Keywords improved in ranking
  • Keywords new to top 10
  • Estimated traffic from keyword improvements
  • Issues found and fixed
  • Backlink growth
  • Next month goals

Formula fields do the math. You spend 10 minutes customizing the narrative; Notion handles the data.

Workflow: Day-to-Day as an SEO Specialist

Monday: Weekly Data Update (30 min)

  1. Pull keyword rankings from Ahrefs/SEMrush for your top 5 clients
  2. Update each keyword's "current rank" field in Notion
  3. Notion auto-calculates: did we improve? Drop? Breakthrough into top 10?
  4. Add any new findings from your audit tools
  5. Flag urgent issues for client communication

Wednesday: Client Check-In (15 min per client)

  1. Open client profile
  2. View all linked keywords (their current ranking status)
  3. Check for breakthroughs or drops
  4. Update any new notes from the conversation
  5. Schedule next review date

End of Month: Generate Report (30 min for 10 clients)

  1. Filter keyword database by client
  2. View "improved this month" and "new to top 10" pages (pre-built)
  3. Copy KPI summary into client report template
  4. Write recommendations based on competitor tracking
  5. Send and mark report as delivered in Notion

Total: 2-3 hours/month on data management and reporting (instead of 6-8 hours in spreadsheets).
