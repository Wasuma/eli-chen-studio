Why Podcasters Need a Notion System

  • Episode planning: 12+ episodes queued months in advance
  • Guest coordination: Booking, contact info, scheduling, prep calls
  • Recording logistics: When to record, tech setup, backup plans
  • Sponsorship deals: Which episodes have sponsors, ad copy, payment tracking

The Notion Podcast Setup: Core Databases

1. Master Episode Database

Create one entry for every episode (past and future):

  • Episode number and title
  • Guest names (if applicable)
  • Episode topic/description
  • Planned publish date
  • Recording date (when you'll record it)
  • Status: ideation, booked, recorded, edited, ready to publish, published
  • Audio duration
  • Sponsor/ads included (yes/no, which sponsor)

Pro tip: Add a "Days until publish" formula. Set a status rule: turn red if less than 7 days and not yet recorded.

2. Guest Directory

Centralized guest database with all their info:

  • Guest name and title
  • Email and phone number
  • Website/social media links
  • Bio (for intro script)
  • Episodes appeared on (relation to episode database)
  • Availability (preferred recording times)
  • Booking status: pending, confirmed, recorded, declined
  • Prep notes (what you discussed pre-interview)

Filter views: "Available guests," "Already appeared," "Pending booking," "Overdue follow-up."

3. Recording Schedule Calendar

Timeline of when episodes will be recorded:

  • Record date/time
  • Guest name(s)
  • Tech setup checklist (mic test, zoom link, backup audio)
  • Pre-recording notes
  • Post-recording status

Calendar view shows your upcoming recording sessions at a glance.

4. Sponsor & Monetization Tracker

Track all sponsor deals and ad reads:

  • Sponsor name and industry
  • Episodes featuring sponsor (relation to episode database)
  • Ad read copy (exact script to use on air)
  • Monthly rate or per-episode rate
  • Payment status: pending, received, overdue
  • Contract terms and renewal date
  • Sponsor link (affiliate or direct)

Quickly see: which episodes have sponsors, when payment is due, how much you're making.

5. Show Notes Template & Tracker

Standardized show notes for every episode:

  • Episode number and title
  • Timestamp-based segments (00:00 intro, 05:30 guest intro, 15:00 main topic, etc.)
  • Key links and resources mentioned
  • Guest social media links and website
  • Sponsor mentions and links
  • Call-to-action (subscribe, leave review, etc.)

Use the same template for every episode. Consistency builds audience trust.

6. Distribution Checklist

Publish-day checklist to ensure nothing gets missed:

  • Episode number
  • Upload to Spotify (date/time completed)
  • Upload to Apple Podcasts (date/time completed)
  • Upload to YouTube (date/time completed)
  • Post to social media (date/time completed)
  • Send email to list (date/time completed)
  • Add show notes to website
  • Post on LinkedIn/Twitter/Instagram

Mark each completed. Nothing falls through the cracks.

7. Analytics & Performance Tracker

Track episode performance over time:

  • Episode number and title
  • Download count (month 1, month 2, month 3, etc.)
  • Listener retention: how far do listeners get?
  • Popular segments (where do people pause/rewind?)
  • Reviews and ratings
  • Notable feedback or comments

Identify: which episode formats resonate? Which guests drive the most downloads? What topics have highest retention?

Workflow: Podcast Production Month-to-Month

First Week of Month: Book Next Wave of Guests (2 hours)

  1. Open guest directory, filter "available" guests
  2. Send 10 booking emails (using pre-built template)
  3. Add them to episode database with status "pending"
  4. Set reminder to follow up in 1 week if no response

Mid-Month: Confirm & Schedule (30 min)

  1. Check guest responses; update booking status to "confirmed"
  2. Add them to recording schedule calendar
  3. Send them pre-interview prep notes (template)

Recording Days: Execute Recording Session (1–2 hours + actual recording)

  1. Check recording schedule calendar
  2. Open guest profile (notes, any special prep)
  3. Run through tech checklist (mic test, zoom link, backup)
  4. Record episode
  5. Update episode status to "recorded"

Post-Recording: Edit & Prepare (2–3 hours, done by editor if you have one)

  1. Edit audio (remove ums, add intro/outro, music)
  2. Create show notes from recording transcript
  3. Update episode status to "ready to publish"

Publish Day: Launch Episode (1 hour)

  1. Open distribution checklist
  2. Upload to Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube (check off each)
  3. Post to social media with show notes link
  4. Send email to list with episode summary + link
  5. Mark episode as "published" in database

Total time per episode: 6-8 hours of active work (spread over 2-3 weeks per episode). Plus actual recording + editing.
