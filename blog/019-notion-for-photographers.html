Is your photography business scattered across email, text messages, and handwritten notes? From client inquiries to session planning to editing timelines to final delivery, photographers need a system to manage the entire client journey. Notion is that system—keeping clients happy and deliverables on track.

The Photography Business Challenge

Every photographer faces the same workflow:

  1. Client inquiry arrives (email, Instagram DM, website form)
  2. Send price quote and availability
  3. Client books session (you confirm date and details)
  4. Send client questionnaire (what do they want? outfit ideas? location preferences?)
  5. Pre-session planning (location scout, equipment check, shot list prep)
  6. Shoot day (capture images, manage client expectations)
  7. Post-shoot (organize images, start editing)
  8. Edit and delivery (send to client for approval, iterate on revisions)
  9. Final delivery (client gets photos, you collect payment)
  10. Follow-up (thank you, request reviews, pitch repeat business)

Managing this for 3-5 concurrent clients without a system leads to:

  • Missed client details ("Wait, didn't they mention their anniversary?...")
  • Editing delays (you lose track of which session is in progress)
  • Lost opportunities (can't remember to follow up for repeat bookings)
  • Disorganized portfolio (which shots got the best engagement?)
  • Payment issues (client forgot they haven't paid, you forgot to follow up)
  • Overwhelm (it's all in your head, and you're stressed)

Notion consolidates the entire workflow into one place. Every client, every session, every deadline, visible at a glance.

What a Notion Photography Workspace Includes

1. Client & Lead Manager

Central database for all clients and prospects. Store: name, phone, email, location, session history, favorite shoot style, preferred editing style, referral source, and personal notes. Quick views show: new leads, booked clients, repeat clients, and high-value clients. Never lose a prospect email again.

2. Session & Booking Calendar

Visual calendar showing all scheduled sessions. Prevent double-booking. For each session: date, time, location, session type (portrait, wedding, product, event, headshots), duration, linked client, number of people, and special requests. Calendar view shows your year at a glance; upcoming sessions alert you to preparation.

3. Client Questionnaire & Preferences

Custom intake form per session type. Capture: client vision, outfit preferences, location ideas, posing preferences, aesthetic inspiration, and special requests. Send to client before the session; they fill it out; you have all the information you need to deliver exactly what they want.

4. Pre-Session Checklist & Planning

Preparation for each session: location scout notes, equipment needed, lighting setup, props, backup locations, outfit suggestions sent to client. Checklist ensures you don't forget anything. Timeline: 2 weeks before prep, 1 week before final check, 3 days before logistics confirmation.

5. Shoot Day Workflow

During the shoot: access shot list, backup ideas, and client preferences on your phone. Post-shoot: log how many images captured, technical notes (settings, focal lengths), any challenges, and what worked well. This feedback improves future sessions.

6. Editing & Delivery Timeline

Track editing status: images uploaded, editing started, editing complete, client review, revisions submitted, final approved. Automatic deadline reminders: "Editing due in 5 days" on a particular date. Never miss a delivery deadline. Client sees expected delivery date when you take the booking.

7. Portfolio & Gallery Management

Curate your best work by genre: portraits, weddings, products, events. Track which clients have approved their images for portfolio use. Store links to published galleries, Instagram posts, website galleries. Analytics: which portfolio shots get the most engagement and inquiries.

8. Payment & Invoice Tracker

Session pricing, deposit status, final payment received, payment date, refunds. Link to client and session for complete payment history. Invoice templates pre-built. Monthly revenue dashboard shows income trends and highest-revenue sessions.

9. Content & Social Media Calendar

Plan Instagram posts, TikToks, Pinterest pins, Reels from your shoot content. Link to sessions and track which clients approved their images for social sharing. Schedule posts, track engagement, identify best-performing content. Social media becomes a marketing tool, not a time drain.

The Photography Workflow in Notion

When a Lead Arrives

  1. Create new Lead record: name, phone, email, session type interest, referral source
  2. Send pricing and availability (all stored in your database for consistency)
  3. If they book, convert Lead to Client, create Session record, send Questionnaire link

Two Weeks Before Session

  1. Notion reminder: "Portrait session with Sarah in 14 days"
  2. Open Pre-Session Checklist: location scout completed? Equipment checked? Backup plan?
  3. Review client questionnaire: outfit preferences, location ideas, posing preferences
  4. Create shot list based on their vision

Shoot Day

  1. Open session detail on your phone: client preferences, shot list, backup ideas
  2. During/after shoot: log number of images captured, technical notes, what worked
  3. Upload images to your editing folder

Editing Phase

  1. Move session status to "Editing Started"
  2. Notion shows you have 5 days remaining to complete editing
  3. 3 days before deadline: automated reminder to prioritize this session
  4. Finish editing, move status to "Editing Complete" — client is automatically notified to review

Client Approval & Delivery

  1. Client reviews images (you send gallery link or PDF)
  2. Log any revision requests in Notion
  3. Revise and re-send; track approval status
  4. Once approved: deliver final images, update payment status, request review
  5. Mark session as "Delivered"

Portfolio & Follow-Up

  1. Select best shots from session for portfolio
  2. Request client approval to use images on social media and portfolio
  3. Add to Instagram calendar: schedule posts for the month
  4. Create follow-up task: "Email client in 3 months — offer 20% repeat booking discount"

Scaling Your Photography Business

As you grow from 2-3 sessions per month to 10-15, Notion scales with you:

  • Hire a second shooter: Invite them to the workspace. They see upcoming sessions, client preferences, shot lists.
  • Hire an editor: Track which sessions are assigned to which editor; manage editing queue.
  • Expand to multiple genres: Create templates for wedding sessions, portraits, products, events—each with custom checklists and workflows.
  • Manage team capacity: See who's booked solid and who has availability; balance workload.
