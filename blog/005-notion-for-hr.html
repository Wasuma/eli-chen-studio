The problem? Maria doesn't need a comprehensive enterprise HR platform designed for 1,000-person corporations. She needs a system that standardizes her processes, ensures nothing falls through the cracks, keeps employees compliant with policies, and doesn't cost $500/month.

That's what Notion does.

What Small Companies Actually Need in HR

Enterprise HR platforms like Workday, SAP, and ADP are overkill for small organizations. They have features no one uses. They cost thousands per month. They lock you into vendor contracts. And they're often harder to use than simpler tools.

What small companies actually need:

  • Standardized onboarding. Every new hire should go through the same process. Someone should own each task. Nothing should be forgotten.
  • Employee directory that's current. You should know who's employed, who's a contractor, who left, where everyone's contact information is, and what department they're in.
  • Policy acknowledgments documented. Employees should read your handbook. You need proof they read it. Compliance requires this.
  • Training records tracked. Who's been trained on what? Whose certifications are expiring? Who needs a refresher?
  • Performance reviews on schedule. Reviews shouldn't be a panic three days before the deadline. You need reminders.
  • Benefits information organized. Who has which health plan? When do open enrollment dates happen? What's each person's coverage?
  • Time off managed smoothly. How many vacation days has each person used? Who's out this week? Can you approve the request quickly?
  • Off-boarding that doesn't miss steps. When someone leaves, you need a checklist to ensure their equipment is returned, their access is removed, and knowledge is handed off.

None of these things are complicated. They just need a system.

How an HR Operating System Works

Imagine having one place where:

Your Hiring and Onboarding is Standardized

A new hire is coming in two weeks. You create an entry in your onboarding database. Notion automatically assigns tasks to the relevant people:

  • IT: Set up laptop, email, Slack account
  • Operations: Get background check, I-9, tax forms, direct deposit
  • Manager: Schedule first week meetings, assign mentor
  • HR: Run through policy acknowledgments, assign benefits forms
  • Equipment: Order office furniture, badge access

Each task has an owner and a due date. Everyone sees their assignments. Nothing falls through the cracks. The new hire doesn't discover on day one that their laptop isn't ready.

Your Employees Have a Complete Record

Every employee has a profile with:

  • Contact information, role, department, manager
  • Start date, contract type (W-2, 1099)
  • Compensation, benefits, emergency contacts
  • Training history and certifications
  • Performance reviews and goals
  • Status (active, on leave, departed)

You can run reports: "Which employees' AWS certifications expire this quarter?" or "Who hasn't taken compliance training yet?"

Your Policies are Documented and Acknowledged

Your company handbook lives in Notion. When you add a new policy, you tag which employees need to review it. You create acknowledgment tasks for each employee. You have proof that everyone read it and agreed to it. This matters for compliance.

Your Performance Reviews Don't Sneak Up

At the start of the year, you create a performance review for each employee on their anniversary date. You get a reminder 3 weeks before. You collect feedback, conduct the review, document the outcome, and set new goals. Everything is in one place, with a complete history of prior reviews.

Your Off-Boarding Process is Consistent

When an employee leaves, you create an offboarding entry. Notion automatically assigns tasks:

  • Manager: Collect knowledge, assign work to others, exit interview
  • IT: Disable access, retrieve equipment, archive data
  • Operations: Final paycheck, insurance COBRA notification
  • HR: Benefits termination, reference letter preparation

You never forget to disable an employee's Slack access again.

A Real Example: Maria's HR System

Before: Maria kept employee records in an Excel spreadsheet that broke whenever she tried to add a formula. Onboarding checklists were in Google Docs (different version for each hire). Benefits info was scattered across emails. Performance reviews were in a shared folder, inconsistently formatted. Time off was tracked in a calendar that no one kept updated. Off-boarding had no checklist — she just remembered things as she went.

After: Maria built a Notion HR workspace with 10 databases:

  1. Employee Directory — All current, past, and contractor employees with complete profiles
  2. New Hire Onboarding — Every new hire goes through the same standardized process
  3. Onboarding Task Tracker — Who's responsible for each task, deadline, completion status
  4. Company Policies — All policies with version control and last-updated dates
  5. Policy Acknowledgments — Track which employees read and acknowledged which policies
  6. Training & Development — Certifications, courses, renewal dates, completion status
  7. Performance Reviews — Annual reviews scheduled on each employee's anniversary, with goals and feedback
  8. Benefits Tracking — Which employees have which health plans, dental, 401k, renewal dates
  9. Time Off / Leave — Vacation days, sick time, unpaid leave, balances, approvals
  10. Off-Boarding Checklist — Standard checklist for employees departing (equipment return, access removal, knowledge handoff)

Now Maria's HR process is streamlined:

First day of quarter: Notion reminds her to schedule performance reviews for employees with anniversaries this quarter.

Hiring manager posts opening: Maria creates a new employee record in her template, triggering the onboarding workflow.

New hire starts: Maria uses the onboarding checklist. IT sees their task immediately. Operations sees theirs. Nothing is forgotten.

Employee takes time off: They request it in Notion. Manager approves it. The time off database is automatically updated. Everyone knows who's out.

Certification expiring: Notion reminds the employee and the manager. No surprises.

Employee leaving: Maria runs the off-boarding checklist. Every task is tracked. Access is removed on day one. Equipment is retrieved.

Time spent on HR admin? Down by 5+ hours per week.

For Different Company Stages

Early Stage (1-10 people)

The employee directory is your main hub. You're still doing hiring and onboarding manually, but now it's documented. When you hire person #11, you have a record of how onboarding worked for people 1-10.

Growth Stage (10-30 people)

Onboarding becomes the focus. You've hired 20 people and realize your process is ad-hoc. Notion standardizes it. Performance reviews become important. Policies need to exist. Benefits need tracking.

Established Stage (30-100+ people)

Everything becomes critical. You can't forget a step in onboarding — you have 50+ people and someone will complain. You need performance review discipline. You need policy acknowledgments for compliance. You need a way to manage off-boarding so you don't accidentally keep paying people or giving them access after they leave.
