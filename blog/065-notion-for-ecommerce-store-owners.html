The E-commerce Manager's Challenge

Running an online store means juggling multiple operational streams simultaneously:

  • Inventory tracked in Shopify, but supplier orders in a spreadsheet
  • Customer feedback scattered: reviews in Shopify, emails, messages in Instagram/TikTok/email
  • Marketing campaigns planned in Google Docs, executed in Mailchimp/Facebook/Shopify, tracked in spreadsheets
  • Sales data in Shopify, but profitability analysis in a separate spreadsheet
  • Supplier relationships: lead times, MOQs, quality issues — tracked in your head or scattered emails
  • Product roadmap (new SKUs, variants, seasonal items) — discussed in Slack, recorded nowhere
  • No single view of business health: "How are we doing this month?"

How to Structure Notion for E-commerce

Core Databases

1. Products & SKUs — Master product database.

  • Product name, SKU, variants (color, size, etc.)
  • Supplier, cost, selling price, profit margin
  • Current stock level, reorder point, lead time
  • Status: active, archived, seasonal, new launch
  • Views: "Low stock," "By supplier," "Best margin," "New products"

2. Suppliers & Vendors — Supplier hub.

  • Supplier name, products they provide, contact info
  • Lead time, MOQ (minimum order quantity), payment terms
  • Pricing history, quality rating, reliability score
  • Active status, alternative suppliers for each product

3. Orders & Fulfillment — Purchase order tracking.

  • Order number, supplier, products, quantities, cost
  • Order date, expected delivery, actual delivery
  • Payment status, issues/discrepancies, tracking number
  • Views: "Pending delivery," "Overdue," "This month"

4. Customers & Reviews — Customer insight hub.

  • Customer name, repeat vs. one-time, lifetime value
  • Purchase history, product reviews/feedback, repeat rate
  • Email preference, location, acquisition channel
  • Views: "Best customers," "At-risk churn," "By acquisition channel"

5. Marketing Campaigns — Campaign tracker.

  • Campaign name, channel (email, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, SMS), type (promotional, seasonal, new product launch)
  • Date, budget, target audience segment
  • Performance: impressions, clicks, conversions, ROI
  • Views: "Active campaigns," "By channel," "Best ROI"

6. Store Metrics & Analytics — Dashboard & KPIs.

  • Daily/weekly revenue, orders, average order value
  • Customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, repeat rate
  • Product performance: top sellers, worst performers, margin analysis
  • Inventory health: stock turns, carrying cost, excess stock

7. Roadmap & Seasonal Planning — Strategic planning.

  • New SKUs or variants planned, seasonal themes (holiday, summer, back-to-school)
  • Market trends and customer feedback driving decisions
  • Timeline for launch, supplier confirmation, inventory need

E-commerce Workflows in Notion

Weekly Inventory Check

  1. View "Low stock" items from Products database
  2. For each, check lead time and sales velocity
  3. Place order with appropriate supplier (quantity based on demand forecast)
  4. Log order in Orders database with expected delivery

Monthly Performance Review

  1. Pull metrics dashboard: revenue, AOV, customer count, profit margin
  2. Analyze by product: which are stars, which are duds?
  3. Check marketing ROI: which campaigns performed best?
  4. Review inventory turnover: any slow-moving stock?
  5. Identify what's working and double down (more inventory, bigger marketing spend)

Seasonal Campaign Planning

  1. Plan campaign: which products, what discount, email copy, social graphics
  2. Log campaign in Marketing database with target audience and budget
  3. Ensure inventory is stocked for expected surge in sales
  4. Post-campaign: track performance metrics and compare to similar past campaigns

Notion Features That Help Store Owners

Inventory-to-Supplier Link

For each product, see current stock level and automatically flag when reorder is needed (based on lead time + safety stock). Click to place order with linked supplier details.

Customer Segmentation

Tag customers by acquisition source and purchase history. Create filtered views (repeat customers, high-value, at-risk churn). Use for targeted marketing campaigns.

Product Performance Dashboard

See revenue, units sold, profit margin, and return rate for each product. Identify best sellers and underperformers. Make inventory and marketing decisions based on data.

Supplier Quality Scorecard

Track on-time delivery rate, quality issues, and pricing consistency for each supplier. Use when deciding to add or drop suppliers or increase/decrease order volume.

Pro Tip: Create a "Business Health" dashboard page that shows today's metrics: revenue, orders, cash on hand, inventory value, and Days of Inventory on Hand (DIOH). Check it daily to spot trends early.
