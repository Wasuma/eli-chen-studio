Notion for Lawyers: Build Your Case Management System

Published March 2026 | 8 minute read

Managing a legal practice means juggling cases, deadlines, clients, documents, and billable hours. Many lawyers and paralegals rely on expensive practice management software like Clio ($50-300/month) or scattered spreadsheets and apps. What if you could consolidate everything into one free system?

Notion can be your entire legal practice management solution.

Why Notion for Legal Practice?

  1. One-time cost — Unlike Clio or MyCase, you buy the template once and own it forever
  2. Complete control — Your data stays with you; no vendor lock-in
  3. Fully customizable — Adapt it to your specific practice areas and workflows
  4. Scalable — Start as a solo practitioner, grow to a small firm

What You Can Track in Notion

Case Management

  • Case details: case number, court, judge, parties, status
  • Statute of limitations tracking with automatic urgency alerts
  • Case timeline and phase tracking
  • Document checklists for pleadings, discovery, evidence
  • Link clients, opposing counsel, and parties to cases

Time & Billing

  • Log billable hours by attorney, by case, by task type
  • Track hourly rates and auto-calculate invoice amounts
  • See unbilled hours at a glance
  • Generate invoices directly from time entries

Deadlines

  • Master deadline tracker with automatic highlighting for urgent dates
  • Calendar view of hearings, depositions, and conferences
  • Deadline categories: motions, filings, court hearings, settlement conferences
  • Track extensions and status

Client Management

  • Client contact information and history
  • Retainer tracking
  • Communication preferences
  • Case portfolio by client
  • Relationship timeline

How to Set It Up

  1. Create a new Notion workspace or add to existing one
  2. Set up core databases: Cases, Clients, Deadlines, Time Tracking, Invoices
  3. Create relationships: link Time Entries to Cases, Cases to Clients, etc.
  4. Build views: filtered views for urgent deadlines, unbilled time, overdue invoices
  5. Customize: add your practice areas, matter types, and hourly rates

Real Example: Solo Family Law Practice

Sarah, a family law attorney, uses Notion to manage 12 active cases. She logs time as she works (2 clicks: case + hours). Each Friday, she exports her unbilled hours, creates invoices, and emails clients. Before Notion, this took 3 hours. Now it takes 30 minutes.

She also watches her statute of limitations tracker obsessively — Notion flags any deadline within 60 days, and she's never missed one.

Key Databases You Need

Cases — The hub. Contains all case information, links to clients, deadlines, documents, and time entries.

Clients — Contact info, retainer amounts, communication preferences, total lifetime value.

Time Entries — Billable hours logged by attorney, case, task type, with automatic rate calculations.

Deadlines — All court dates, filing deadlines, motions, with automatic urgency sorting.

Invoices — Generated from time entries, track payment status, manage receivables.

Documents — Track required documents, pleadings, discovery, evidence, filing status.

Witnesses — Fact and expert witnesses, deposition scheduling, contact info.

Tips for Legal Practitioners

  1. Set your hourly rates in the template so invoices calculate automatically
  2. Use custom fields for your specific practice area: Family law? Add "Custody Status". Corporate? Add "Deal Stage"
  3. Create filtered views for your most-used workflows (Overdue Deadlines, Unbilled Hours, This Week's Appearances)
  4. Link everything — the power of Notion is in the relationships between databases
  5. Template checklists for routine case types (settlement agreement, discovery request response, etc.)

Limitations to Know

  • Notion is not a case management system in the traditional sense; it won't integrate with court filing systems
  • You'll still need separate billing software if you take credit card payments
  • Electronic signature and document assembly require separate tools

But for organizing your practice, tracking deadlines, and managing billing? Notion is powerful and affordable.
