What You Can Manage in Notion

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

  • Customer database: name, email, phone, location, company, contact history
  • Deal pipeline: prospects, active deals, closed deals, deal value
  • Interaction history: emails sent, calls made, meetings scheduled
  • Contract tracking: contract date, renewal date, terms
  • Auto-calculate: customer lifetime value, win rate, average deal size

Project Management

  • Projects: name, client, status, deadline, team members
  • Tasks: what needs to be done, assigned to whom, due date, priority
  • Progress tracking: % complete, blockers, dependencies
  • Timeline view: Gantt chart to see project schedule
  • Calendar view: deadline overview for the week/month

Product & Inventory Management

  • Product catalog: SKU, name, description, cost, selling price, quantity in stock
  • Auto-calculate: profit per unit, stock status (in stock vs. low stock vs. out of stock)
  • Orders: what customer ordered what, when, and status
  • Inventory alerts: auto-flag when stock drops below minimum
  • Supplier tracking: vendor contact, lead time, cost per unit

Financial Tracking

  • Revenue: all sales by date, customer, product/service
  • Expenses: vendor, amount, category, date, receipt link
  • Invoices: auto-generate from projects/orders, track payment status
  • Monthly dashboard: revenue vs. expenses, profit, growth rate
  • Tax tracking: categorize expenses for tax season

Team & HR Management

  • Team roster: name, role, email, phone, start date, hourly rate/salary
  • Responsibility matrix: who owns which customers/projects/functions
  • Time off: vacation, sick days, personal days
  • Performance notes: goals, achievements, feedback

Core Databases for Small Business

Essential Tables

  • Customers — Name, contact, company, location, deal history
  • Orders/Deals — Customer, product/service, amount, date, status
  • Projects — Name, client, deadline, team, % complete
  • Tasks — Assigned to, due date, project, priority, status
  • Products/Services — Name, cost, price, inventory quantity
  • Invoices — Customer, amount, date, payment status
  • Expenses — Vendor, amount, category, date
  • Team Members — Name, role, email, phone, start date

How to Build Your Business Operating System

  1. Start with your core databases: Customers, Projects/Orders, Products, Invoices
  2. Link everything together:
    • Customers link to Orders
    • Orders link to Products and Invoices
    • Projects link to Customers and Team Members
  3. Create essential views for your team:
    • Sales pipeline (prospects → deals → closed)
    • This month's projects (sorted by deadline)
    • Inventory dashboard (low stock items)
    • Revenue & expense summary
    • Team workload (tasks per person)
  4. Build formulas that auto-calculate:
    • Invoice total from line items
    • Profit per order (price - cost)
    • Days overdue for invoices
    • Customer lifetime value
  5. Set up automation with Zapier:
    • New customer email → create customer record in Notion
    • New order → auto-create invoice
    • Invoice due date approaching → send reminder

Alex runs a small Etsy shop selling handmade jewelry. Using Notion, he:

  • Tracks inventory: 200+ SKUs, auto-alerts when stock hits 5 units
  • Manages orders: customer info, shipping status, return tracking

Before Notion, Alex used 6 different apps + spreadsheets. He couldn't quickly answer questions like "How much profit did we make last month?" or "Which products are selling best?" Now he sees it at a glance. His employee can see the daily tasks without email ping-pong. Alex is able to scale to $150k without hiring a full-time operations person.

Scaling Beyond Notion

  • Under $50k revenue — Notion is sufficient; no additional tools needed
  • $50k-$200k revenue — Notion + Zapier for automation + Stripe for payments
  • Over $200k revenue — Consider upgrading to dedicated tools (Salesforce, Shopify) while keeping Notion for operations
  • Team management — Notion works well up to 5-10 people; larger teams benefit from dedicated project management tools
