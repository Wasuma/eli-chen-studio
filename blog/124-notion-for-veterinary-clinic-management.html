How to Organize Notion for Veterinary Clinic Management

Master Dashboard

Your clinic's command center showing:

  • Today's appointments: Timeline view of all scheduled appointments
  • Overdue vaccinations: Count of patients needing vaccine reminders (auto-generated)
  • Pending results: Lab results waiting for follow-up with clients
  • Surgery schedule: Week's surgical cases with pre-op checklist status
  • Outstanding invoices: Payments pending from clients
  • Staff status: Who's on today, break schedule, vacation days

Patient Database (Core)

Master pet database with:

  • Pet info: Name, species (dog, cat, rabbit), breed, DOB, color/markings, microchip ID
  • Owner info: Name, phone, email, address (linked to owner database)
  • Medical profile: Known allergies, chronic conditions, previous surgeries, current medications
  • Vaccination status: Last rabies date, DHPP status, FVRCP (cats), heartworm test, flea/tick status
  • Weight tracking: Current weight, weight history (spot obesity early)
  • Medical notes: Summary of last 2-3 visits for quick reference
  • Views: "All active patients," "By species," "Overdue vaccinations," "Surgery candidates," "By veterinarian"

Appointment Scheduler

Integrated scheduling with patient history:

  • Date & time: Day, time slot, duration
  • Patient: Linked to patient database (auto-populate weight, medical history, last visit)
  • Appointment type: Wellness exam, vaccination, surgery, dental, emergency, follow-up
  • Veterinarian: Which vet is assigned
  • Notes: Chief complaint, special instructions, owner requests
  • Status: Scheduled, completed, no-show, cancelled, rescheduled
  • Invoice linked: Services performed and charges (filled in after visit)

Medical Records Database

Visit documentation linked to patient:

  • Visit date & veterinarian: Who saw the patient when
  • Presenting complaint: Why the owner brought the pet in
  • Vitals: Temperature, heart rate, respiratory rate, blood pressure
  • Physical exam: Structured fields (eyes, ears, mouth, abdomen, joints, skin, etc.)
  • Diagnosis: Primary diagnosis and differential diagnosis
  • Treatment plan: Medications, dosages, frequency, duration
  • Lab results: Blood work, urinalysis, imaging reports (with links to files)
  • Follow-up: Next appointment date, recheck labs, owner instructions

Vaccination Tracker

Automatic alerts for vaccination due dates:

  • Database with vaccination schedule by species (dogs, cats, rabbits)
  • Each patient's record shows: Rabies (due date), DHPP (due date), FVRCP (due date), Heartworm (test due), Flea/tick (refill due)
  • Create a view showing "Overdue vaccinations" (no filter, just overdue animals)
  • Export overdue list to send vaccine reminder emails to owners
  • Template email: "Hi John, Max is due for his rabies vaccination. Call us to schedule an appointment."

Surgical Log

Track all procedures with pre-op and post-op details:

  • Procedure type: Spay, neuter, dental cleaning, mass removal, fracture repair, emergency surgery
  • Patient & date: Which patient, scheduled date, actual surgical date
  • Surgeon & staff: Vet and surgical technician assigned
  • Pre-op checklist: Pre-surgical exam complete, bloodwork normal, fasting confirmed
  • Anesthesia: Drug used, dose, pre-op medications, intra-op monitoring
  • Surgical details: Procedure performed, time, complications, notes
  • Post-op instructions: Activity restriction, medication, bandage care, recheck date
  • Recovery status: Recovery progress notes, complication tracking

Invoicing & Payment Tracking

Generate bills and track payment status:

  • Service database: Services offered (wellness exam $50, vaccinations $25, dental cleaning $300, lab work $75)
  • Invoice database: Linked to appointment, shows services rendered and charges
  • Total due: Auto-calculated from services selected
  • Payment method: Cash, credit card, check, insurance claim
  • Payment status: Pending, partially paid, paid in full, insurance pending
  • Client balance: Running total of payments and outstanding invoices
  • Past due alerts: Invoices 30+ days past due highlighted in red
Pro Tip: Create a "services offered" database with price list. When you link services to invoices, prices auto-populate. You can update prices once and they reflect everywhere.

FAQs for Veterinary Clinic Managers

Can I migrate my existing patient records into Notion?

Yes! If you have records in Excel or another system, you can export as CSV and import into Notion. Manual entry works too but is time-intensive for large clinics. Start with new patients and gradually migrate old records.

Is Notion secure enough for patient medical records?

Notion has encryption at rest and in transit, similar to cloud-based email. For HIPAA compliance, consult with your lawyer, but many small clinics use Notion successfully. Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication.

Can I integrate Notion with scheduling software my clients use?

Notion doesn't natively integrate with third-party scheduling software, but you can use Zapier to connect Notion to Calendar apps or email. Manual appointment entry works well for small clinics.

What if I have multiple veterinarians? Can they all edit the database?

Absolutely! Notion allows multiple users to collaborate in real-time. Each vet can see the full patient database and update notes. You control permissions (who can edit, who can only view).

Can I use Notion from an exam room on a tablet?

Yes! Notion mobile app works on tablets and phones. Many clinics use iPad in the exam room to pull up patient history and document notes during the visit.
