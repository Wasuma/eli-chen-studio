Notion for Nonprofits: Manage Grants, Donors & Volunteers

Published March 2026 | 10 minute read

Nonprofits wear multiple hats: securing funding, managing volunteers, serving beneficiaries, reporting impact. The typical nonprofit cobbles together separate systems: spreadsheets for donors, email for grant deadlines, a database for volunteer hours, another system for impact metrics.

No single view of your fundraising funnel. No automated deadline alerts. No way to see which donors funded which programs. No quick answers to board questions.

Notion can be your unified fundraising and operations system.

The Nonprofit Challenge

Nonprofits operate on tight budgets. Expensive CRM software ($100-500/month) is out of reach. So organizations use free tools that don't talk to each other:

  • Spreadsheets for donor tracking (error-prone, hard to search)
  • Google Calendar for grant deadlines (easy to miss)
  • Volunteer management forms (scattered data)
  • Email for grant communication (impossible to track status)
  • Manual calculations for impact reports (time-consuming)

The result: grant deadlines slip. Donors aren't followed up with. Volunteer hours aren't tracked. Impact reports are built last-minute and inaccurate.

What Nonprofits Actually Need

Donor Relationship Management

Your donors are your lifeline. You need to know:

  • Who they are and how to reach them
  • How much they've given (lifetime value)
  • What projects they care about
  • When to reach out next (anniversary of last gift, matching gift opportunity, new program launch)
  • Communication preferences (email vs. phone, newsletter vs. personal outreach)

Grant Management

Grants are time-sensitive and high-stakes:

  • Grant name, funder, deadline, award amount
  • Application status (researching → LOI submitted → full proposal submitted → awarded/rejected)
  • Reporting requirements and due dates
  • Which program/project the grant funds
  • Automated alerts for approaching deadlines

Volunteer Coordination

Volunteers are often more valuable than money:

  • Volunteer roster with contact info and skills
  • Hours logged per volunteer and per project
  • Project assignments and upcoming shifts
  • Annual impact reporting (1,000 volunteer hours = X client hours of service)

Impact & Program Tracking

Your board and donors want evidence:

  • Clients served by program
  • Outcomes (behavior change, skill development, housing secured, etc.)
  • Budget spent vs. projected per program
  • Donor funding linked to outcomes (show donors the impact of their money)

How Notion Solves This

One System for All Funding & Operations

Build a Notion workspace with these databases:

  • Donor Database — Complete contact history, giving history, preferences, next outreach date
  • Gift Log — Every donation recorded with donor, amount, project funded, tax receipt status
  • Grant Pipeline — Status of each grant from research to reporting (never miss a deadline again)
  • Grant Reports — Track which reports are due when and what metrics you need to gather
  • Volunteer Database — Volunteer roster with skills, availability, project history
  • Volunteer Hours — Log hours by volunteer, project, date; auto-calculate monthly/annual totals
  • Project Tracker — Which donors fund which programs? What's the budget? How many clients served?
  • Impact Dashboard — Board report view: total funds raised, volunteer hours, clients served, outcomes achieved

Real Example: Mid-Size Nonprofit

Sarah directs a nonprofit serving homeless youth. She manages 3 full-time staff, 12 active volunteers, donors ranging from $25 annual gifts to $50,000 corporate sponsors, and 2 active grant applications.

Before Notion: Sarah tracked donors in a spreadsheet (outdated). Grant deadlines were on her calendar but she'd miss emails. Volunteer hours were scattered across text messages and forms. Board meetings meant 4 hours of report building.

After Notion:

  • She sees at a glance: 47 active donors, $165,000 in lifetime giving, 3 major gift prospects due for cultivation
  • Grant deadlines are in her Notion dashboard with automatic reminders 3 weeks in advance
  • Volunteer hours are logged real-time; she can see that volunteers contributed 320 hours this month
  • Her board report pulls data automatically: "Served 84 youth, received $127,000 in donations, 320 volunteer hours, outcomes: 42 youth housed, 38 in school, 28 employed"
  • She spends 2 hours building a board report (not 4), and it's always current

Building Your Nonprofit Notion System

Setup Process

  1. Duplicate the template (one click)
  2. Import current donors from your spreadsheet (CSV import)
  3. Add your active grants with deadlines and status
  4. Create volunteer roster with current volunteers
  5. Define your projects/programs and connect them to donors and volunteers
  6. Set up monthly report view that auto-calculates: funds raised, expenses, volunteer hours, clients served

Pro Tips

  • Link donors to grants: See which major donors could fund upcoming grant opportunities
  • Automate reminders: Set follow-up dates for donor outreach (30 days after gift, anniversary, quarterly check-in)
  • Track grant success rates: Over time, you'll see which funders you have the best relationship with
  • Map volunteer skills to needs: When a project needs someone, match by skill
  • Build outcomes tracking: Link clients served to specific programs and donors so you can tell impact stories

Why Notion Beats Nonprofit CRM Software

  • Cost: $10/month for team plan vs. $100-500/month for nonprofit CRM
  • Customization: Design exactly what your nonprofit needs (no bloated features you'll never use)
  • Data ownership: Your donor data belongs to you (not locked in a vendor's system)
  • All-in-one: One system for donors + grants + volunteers + impact (not 4 separate tools)
  • Board reporting: Pull impact data instantly for board meetings and annual reports

Getting Started

You can have a complete nonprofit operations system running in a day:

  1. Get the template and duplicate into Notion
  2. Spend 30 minutes importing your current donor list
  3. Add your active grants and deadlines
  4. Invite staff to the shared workspace
  5. Start logging donations, grant submissions, and volunteer hours

Within a week, you have automated reminders, visibility into your entire funding pipeline, and the ability to answer "How much did donors give to Program X?" in 10 seconds.
