What You Can Manage in Notion

Grant Management

  • Grant pipeline: opportunities, applications in progress, awarded grants, closed opportunities
  • Grant details: funder, deadline, amount requested, deliverables, reporting requirements
  • Application checklist: required documents, submission status, review notes
  • Grant timeline: award date, reporting dates, final report due
  • Status tracking: which team member is responsible, current phase
  • Auto-highlight: grants with deadlines approaching in next 30 days

Donor Management (CRM)

  • Donor database: name, contact, giving history, interests, capacity
  • Interaction log: calls, emails, meetings, thank-you cards sent
  • Giving history: all donations with date, amount, designation
  • Lifetime value: total given, average gift size, giving trends
  • Major donor pipeline: prospects, stewardship touches, ask amounts
  • Linked to grant funding: see which funders/donors support which programs

Volunteer Management

  • Volunteer roster: name, contact, skills, availability, background check status
  • Volunteer assignments: event, role, date, hours
  • Hours tracking: total hours logged, contribution value, trends
  • Skills matrix: which volunteers can do which roles
  • Event coordination: who's coming, what they're doing, emergency contacts

Program Management

  • Program inventory: name, goal, budget, funder, lead staff
  • Program activities: workshops, events, services delivered, dates
  • Participant tracking: who benefited from programs, demographics
  • Budget per program: expenses, grants, revenue, remaining balance

Impact Measurement

  • Outcome metrics: participants served, lives changed, dollars mobilized
  • Goals vs. actuals: target number served vs. actual impact
  • Success stories: testimonials and case studies linked to programs
  • Dashboard: visual summary of annual impact for reports and donor communication

Core Databases for Nonprofits

Essential Tables

  • Grants — Funder, amount, deadline, status, reporting requirements
  • Donors — Name, contact, gift history, interests, capacity
  • Donations — Date, donor, amount, program designation
  • Volunteers — Name, contact, skills, availability
  • Volunteer Hours — Volunteer, event, hours, role
  • Programs — Name, goal, budget, funder, lead staff
  • Program Participants — Name, program, outcome metrics
  • Events — Date, type, volunteers needed, budget

How to Build Your Nonprofit System

  1. Create core databases: Grants, Donors, Programs, Volunteers, Events
  2. Link everything:
    • Donations link to Donors and Programs
    • Grants link to Programs (which programs does this grant fund?)
    • Program Participants link to Programs and Outcomes
    • Volunteer Hours link to Events and Programs
  3. Create views for each team member's role:
    • Development: grant pipeline, upcoming deadlines, donor interactions
    • Programs: participant tracking, volunteer needs, event schedules
    • Executive Director: impact dashboard, financial summary, donor status
    • Volunteers: their upcoming events, hours logged, appreciation
  4. Build formulas:
    • Total donations per donor (lifetime value)
    • Total volunteer hours contributed
    • Grant success rate (awarded ÷ applied)
    • Cost per person served (budget ÷ participants)
  5. Create a public impact dashboard to share with board and donors

Real Example: Small Nonprofit with 4 Staff

The Community Garden Initiative has 4 staff members managing 3 grant programs, 200+ volunteers, and 2,000 participants annually. Using Notion they:

  • Track 8 active grants with a dashboard showing all deadlines (never miss one)
  • Manage 200+ volunteers: skills inventory, event assignments, hours tracking
  • Monitor program outcomes: participants served, gardens created, families trained
  • Maintain donor database: 150 donors, giving history, personalized stewardship
  • Share impact dashboard with board showing: participants served (2,000), volunteer hours (4,500), gardens created (45)

Before Notion, the Executive Director spent 20% of her time on administrative tasks (finding files, chasing information). Now 80% of her time is on mission work. Staff collaboration is seamless, and donors see a professional, data-driven organization.
