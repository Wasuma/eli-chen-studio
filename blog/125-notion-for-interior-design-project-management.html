The Interior Designer's Project Management Challenge

Managing interior design projects is complex. You're coordinating:

  • Multiple clients at different design phases (discovery, concepts, selections, installation)
  • Design boards, mood boards, color palettes, material selections from 10+ vendors
  • Budget tracking (estimated vs. actual spending)
  • Vendor & contractor coordination (lead times, delivery schedules, installation dates)
  • Client approvals and feedback at each phase
  • Timeline management (design deadlines, material delivery, contractor schedules)
  • Invoicing (design fees, material markup, labor costs)
  • Before/after photos for portfolio building

Most designers juggle spreadsheets, email threads, Pinterest boards, and scattered notes. This creates delays, cost overruns, missed approvals, and unhappy clients. You need a system that keeps everything organized and transparent.

How to Set Up Notion for Interior Design Projects

Master Project Dashboard

Your design command center showing:

  • Active projects: Timeline view showing all projects and their current phase
  • Budget status: Projects on budget vs. over budget at a glance
  • Pending approvals: Clients waiting to approve design selections or changes
  • Vendor delivery dates: Upcoming furniture and material deliveries
  • Upcoming installations: Contractor schedule for next 4 weeks
  • Team workload: How many projects each designer is managing

Project Hub Database

Master project information at a glance:

  • Project info: Address, client name, phone, email, project type (residential kitchen, commercial office, etc.)
  • Timeline: Start date, estimated completion, design deadline, installation deadline
  • Budget: Total project budget, estimated spend, contingency percentage
  • Current phase: Discovery, Concept, Selection, Installation, or Complete
  • Design style: Coastal modern, industrial, minimalist, transitional, etc. (helps with design board references)
  • Scope: Rooms included (master bedroom, living room, kitchen) and what's in scope (full design, styling only, consultation)
  • Assigned designer: Which designer is handling the project

Design Phases Tracker

Break projects into phases with approval gates:

  • Discovery & Consultation
    • Initial meeting notes, client vision, budget discussion, style preferences
    • Measurements and photos collected
    • Mood board started
    • Status: To start → In progress → Approval pending → Complete
  • Concept Design
    • Mood boards created, color palettes designed
    • Layout sketches and floor plan concepts
    • Concept presentation to client
    • Client approval needed before moving to selection
  • Material & Furniture Selection
    • Specific products selected (furniture, paint, flooring, accessories)
    • Client approves each selection
    • Quotes obtained from vendors
    • Orders placed with lead times documented
  • Installation Management
    • Contractor schedule confirmed
    • Material delivery coordination
    • Installation progress tracking
    • Photo documentation during installation
  • Final Walkthrough & Portfolio
    • Final photos and styling
    • Client satisfaction review
    • Portfolio documentation
    • Project archival

Design Board Library

Central location for all design inspiration and selections:

  • Mood boards — Title, description, design style, images (links or attachments)
  • Color palettes — Hex codes, mood, complementary colors, where each color is used
  • Material swatches — Name, type (paint, flooring, fabric), color, finish, price per unit
  • Furniture selections — Name, vendor, price, link to product, lead time, dimensions, fabric/material
  • Client approvals — Checkbox for each selection: "Client approved" (great for phase tracking)

Vendor & Contractor Directory

Manage your trusted suppliers and service providers:

  • Vendor info: Name, phone, email, website, specialty (paint, flooring, upholstery, lighting)
  • Service rates: Hourly rate, project rate, minimum, lead time guarantee
  • Availability: Current capacity, typical lead times, quickest turnaround
  • Reviews from past projects: Quality, professionalism, communication, reliability
  • Current & past projects: Link to projects where this vendor was used

Budget Tracker

Monitor spending and prevent cost overruns:

  • Budget categories: Design fees, furniture, paint, flooring, labor, lighting, accessories, contingency
  • Estimated cost: For each category
  • Actual cost: As purchases are made
  • Variance: Auto-calculated (actual - estimated). Green = under budget, red = over budget
  • Contingency usage: Track if over budget and how much contingency is used
  • Vendor quotes: Link to vendor quotes for comparison before ordering

Installation Schedule

Coordinate contractor and material delivery:

  • Week 1: Paint prep and execution (painter contact: 555-1234)
  • Week 2: Flooring delivery and installation (contractor, lead times
  • Week 3: Furniture delivery and placement (delivery date: 3/25, contact: vendor)
  • Week 4: Final styling, lighting installation, accessories placement
  • Contractor coordination: Arrival time, project duration, any special access needed
  • Photo checkpoints: Take before/after photos at each phase completion

Client Communication Log

Track all decisions, approvals, and feedback:

  • Meeting notes and decisions made
  • Design approvals obtained (date and what was approved)
  • Client change requests and how they were addressed
  • Feedback received and designer response
  • Follow-up action items and deadlines
  • This log is essential for clarifying scope changes and billing disputes

Before/After Portfolio Gallery

Document projects for portfolio and social sharing:

  • Project name (Master Bedroom Transformation, Kitchen Remodel, etc.)
  • Room/space (Master bedroom, living room, kitchen, office)
  • Before photos — Attachment field for multiple before images
  • After photos — Attachment field for multiple after images
  • Design story — Description of the design approach and what changed
  • Client permission — Checkbox: "Client approved for portfolio/social sharing"
  • Portfolio tags — Design style, room type, budget range (helps with website portfolio filtering)
Pro Tip: Create a shareable read-only "Portfolio" view in Notion that displays all approved before/after projects. You can embed this on your website or share the link with prospective clients.

Advanced Features for Scaling Design Agencies

Client-Facing Portal (Shareable View)

Create a read-only project view that clients can access:

  • Project timeline showing current phase and next deadlines
  • Design selections (materials, furniture, colors) with photos
  • Budget summary (estimated vs. actual, not detailed pricing)
  • Installation schedule (when contractors are coming)
  • Approval checkboxes (clients can see what's pending their approval)
  • Client cannot see:
    • Your design fees or vendor pricing
    • Vendor contact info or rates
    • Notes or sensitive communications

Invoice & Payment Tracking

Generate invoices and track client payments:

  • Invoice database — Services rendered (design fee, project management), materials purchased, labor costs
  • Payment schedule — Deposit due, mid-project payment, final payment
  • Payment status — Pending, partially paid, paid in full
  • Material invoices — Link vendor invoices for material costs you're marking up

Team Collaboration (For Agencies)

If you have multiple designers:

  • Assign project to specific designer
  • View team workload by designer
  • Shared design board library and vendor directory
  • Comment and feedback on projects for design critiques

FAQs for Interior Designers

Can I share the client portal without revealing my design fees?

Yes! Create a shareable read-only view that shows timeline, design selections, and budget overview but hides your fee structure and vendor pricing. Clients see transparency without seeing your profit margins.

What if my client wants to make changes mid-project?

Update the communication log with the change request, note any impact on timeline/budget, and get client approval. The log becomes proof of what was approved vs. what was a scope change (useful for change orders).

How do I use this for multiple concurrent projects?

Create a new project entry for each client. Use the dashboard to see all projects at once. Filter by designer, budget status, or phase to manage workload.

Can I embed this on my website to show portfolio?

Notion allows you to embed shareable views on websites. Create a "portfolio" view showing before/after projects and embed it on your portfolio page.

What if I need to send invoices to clients?

Create invoices in Notion with detailed line items (design fee, materials, labor). Export as PDF and email to clients. Notion doesn't have built-in invoice sending but works with PDF export.
