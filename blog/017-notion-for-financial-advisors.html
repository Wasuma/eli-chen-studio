  • Client net worth, risk profiles, and financial goals

Spreadsheets scatter this information across dozens of tabs. Email buries critical decisions. Multiple logins fragment your workflow.

Notion consolidates everything into one searchable, relational database. Find any client detail in seconds. See their complete financial picture instantly. Never miss a compliance deadline.

What a Notion Financial Advisory Workspace Includes

1. Client Master Database

Your single source of truth for every client. Store contact info, family members, income, net worth, risk profile, investment timeline, life milestones, and key dates. Tag clients by category (high-net-worth, prospects, review due soon). Quick filters show which clients need outreach or reviews.

2. Portfolio & Holdings Tracker

Link each client to their asset allocation: stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds, real estate, business interests, alternatives. Track cost basis, current value, performance, dividend income, and rebalancing needs. Automatic calculation of total portfolio value and asset allocation percentages. Spot underallocated or overconcentrated positions at a glance.

3. Financial Plan Database

Store each client's financial plan: retirement income analysis, tax strategy summary, estate planning notes, insurance needs analysis, college funding plan. Link to client records. Track plan version and last review date. Know exactly what you've planned for each client.

4. Tax Planning Calendar

Quarterly estimated tax tracking, year-end tax-loss harvesting opportunities, charitable giving analysis, required minimum distributions (RMDs), and tax law changes relevant to each client tier. Calendar view shows all tax deadlines for the year. Never miss a planning opportunity.

5. Client Meeting Log

Document every client meeting: date, attendees, topics discussed, decisions made, action items assigned. Link to the client for complete relationship history. Filter for outstanding action items. Annual review tracking shows which clients are due for planning conversations.

6. Compliance & Regulatory Calendar

Track SEC/FINRA deadlines, form reviews (ADV, 4506-C, etc.), continuing education requirements, audit schedules, suitability documentation. Annual views ensure nothing slips through the cracks. Automated reminders for upcoming deadlines.

7. Prospect Pipeline

Manage leads from initial contact through onboarding. Track referral source (key for understanding where clients come from), initial meeting date, proposal status, signed documents, first deposit date. Conversion metrics show your sales effectiveness.

8. Performance & Fee Tracking

Monitor AUM by client, investment performance vs. benchmarks, fee calculations (percentage of assets or flat fee), and billing records. Monthly revenue reports with client-level detail. Understand profitability by client tier.

How to Use Notion as a Financial Advisor

Daily Workflow

  1. Open Notion each morning to see your "Upcoming Meetings" and "Action Items Due" views
  2. For new client inquiries, add to Prospect Pipeline and assign follow-up date
  3. During client meetings, open their client record and add notes directly into Meeting Log
  4. Capture any portfolio changes, life events, or action items in real-time
  5. End of week: review all action items, update compliance calendar for upcoming deadlines

Quarterly Workflow

  1. Run Notion report: "Clients Due for Annual Review" (automatic based on last meeting date)
  2. Review portfolio performance by client; identify rebalancing or tax-loss harvesting opportunities
  3. Check tax planning calendar for Q3 estimated tax and year-end planning alerts
  4. Update fee tracking; generate revenue reports by client and asset tier

Annual Workflow

  1. Compliance calendar shows all annual deadlines (form reviews, suitability docs, etc.)
  2. Complete systematic client reviews; document in Meeting Log
  3. Tax planning database updated with new tax law changes for next year
  4. Generate annual performance reports using portfolio performance data

The Privacy & Security Question

Is client financial data safe in Notion? Notion encryption is industry-standard. However, consider these best practices:

  • Store client names and contact info only; don't store actual account numbers or sensitive passwords
  • Store holdings and portfolio data at the summary level (asset allocation, totals), not account-by-account details
  • Keep detailed account information in your broker's secure portal; Notion is your planning and relationship hub
  • Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication on your Notion account
  • Limit Notion access to authorized team members only

Notion's encryption is comparable to many financial software platforms, but ultimate responsibility for data security lies with you.
