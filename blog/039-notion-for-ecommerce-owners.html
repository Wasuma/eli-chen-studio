Notion for E-commerce Store Owners: Complete Business Operations Dashboard

Manage inventory, orders, customers, and suppliers from a single unified workspace

TL;DR: E-commerce store owners juggle multiple platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy, Amazon), fragmented inventory data, supplier relationships, customer support, and business metrics scattered across different dashboards. This guide shows you how to build a Notion workspace that centralizes operations, automates order tracking, manages inventory across channels, and gives you real-time business intelligence—so you can focus on growing revenue instead of fighting tool sprawl.

The E-commerce Owner's Challenge

Running an online store means:

  • Multi-channel chaos: Inventory on Shopify doesn't sync with Etsy or Amazon; you oversell constantly
  • Order blindness: Orders scattered across Shopify admin, email notifications, and manual spreadsheets
  • Supplier fragmentation: Lead times, MOQs, pricing for 5-20 suppliers tracked in separate emails and notes
  • Customer data waste: Repeat customer info forgotten; no historical context on their purchases or issues
  • Metrics scattered: Revenue in Stripe, inventory in Google Sheets, customer emails in Mailchimp, analytics in Shopify admin
  • Returns & refunds confusion: No systematic tracking of why products are returned or refund status
  • Team coordination gaps: If you have fulfillment help or customer service staff, they lack visibility into order status and inventory

Why Notion Solves This for E-commerce

Notion becomes your central business dashboard because it:

  • Syncs inventory across channels: Central inventory database feeds into all platforms; prevents overselling
  • Unifies orders: Auto-import from Shopify, Etsy, Amazon via Zapier; all orders in one place
  • Manages suppliers: Track pricing, lead times, MOQs, and reorder schedules in one database
  • Preserves customer data: Every customer's lifetime value, purchase history, and preferences recorded
  • Provides real-time metrics: Revenue, profit margin, inventory turnover, and customer acquisition cost in one dashboard
  • Tracks returns systematically: Reason for return, refund status, and trend analysis
  • Enables team collaboration: Shared views for fulfillment team, customer service, and management

The Notion System: Core Architecture for E-commerce

1. Product Inventory Database

Fields:

  • Product Name: Text
  • SKU: Text (unique identifier)
  • Description: Rich text
  • Category: Select (electronics, apparel, home, etc.)
  • Unit Cost: Currency
  • Selling Price: Currency
  • Margin %: Formula ((Selling Price - Unit Cost) / Selling Price)
  • Quantity on Hand: Number (synced from Shopify/WooCommerce)
  • Reorder Point: Number (when to reorder)
  • Lead Time (days): Number
  • Supplier: Relation to Supplier database
  • Sales This Month: Rollup count
  • Revenue This Month: Rollup sum
  • Status: Select (In Stock, Low Stock, Out of Stock, Discontinued)

2. Orders Database

Fields:

  • Order ID: Text (auto-import from Shopify/Amazon/Etsy)
  • Order Date: Calendar
  • Customer: Relation to Customer database
  • Products Ordered: Relation to Inventory database
  • Quantity: Number
  • Order Total: Currency
  • Status: Status field (Pending, Shipped, Delivered, Cancelled)
  • Platform: Select (Shopify, Etsy, Amazon, WooCommerce, Direct)
  • Tracking Number: Text
  • Customer Email: Email (quick reply reference)
  • Fulfillment Date: Date field
  • Notes: Rich text (gift message, special instructions)

3. Customer Database

Fields:

  • Customer Name: Text
  • Email: Email
  • Phone: Phone number
  • Location: Text (city, state)
  • Total Lifetime Value: Currency (rollup of all orders)
  • Total Orders: Rollup count
  • Last Order Date: Rollup of most recent order
  • Average Order Value: Formula (total value / number of orders)
  • Customer Segment: Select (New, Regular, VIP, At-Risk)
  • Preferred Products: Relation to products they've bought
  • Order History: Relation to Orders database
  • Notes: Rich text (preferences, communication style)

4. Supplier & Inventory Management

Fields:

  • Supplier Name: Text
  • Contact Email: Email
  • Phone: Phone
  • Products Supplied: Relation to Inventory
  • Unit Cost Per Item: Currency
  • MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity): Number
  • Lead Time (days): Number
  • Payment Terms: Text (Net 30, upfront, etc.)
  • Last Order Date: Date
  • Reliability Score: Text (on-time delivery %)

5. Returns & Refunds Tracker

Fields:

  • Return ID: Text
  • Original Order: Relation to Orders
  • Product Returned: Relation to Inventory
  • Return Reason: Select (Defective, Wrong Size, Wrong Item, Changed Mind, Damaged)
  • Refund Amount: Currency
  • Status: Status field (Return Received, Inspected, Refunded, Restocked)
  • Return Date: Date
  • Refund Issued Date: Date

6. Business Metrics Dashboard

Aggregated views showing:

  • Total revenue this month
  • Total expenses (COGS)
  • Gross profit margin
  • Average order value
  • Customer acquisition cost
  • Top 10 products by revenue
  • Return rate (returns / orders)
  • Inventory turnover by product

Pro Workflows

Automatic Inventory Alerts

Use Zapier to check Inventory database daily:

  • Products hitting reorder point auto-send email to supplier with suggested order
  • Out of stock items auto-pause on Shopify/Etsy

Multi-Channel Order Sync

Zapier pulls orders from Shopify, Etsy, Amazon into single Orders database:

  • All orders visible in one chronological view

Common Questions

Q: Can Notion sync with Shopify automatically?

A: Yes via Zapier. Orders auto-import from Shopify to Notion daily. Cost: $15-30/month for Zapier.

Q: How do I update inventory when I sell products?

A: Use Zapier to auto-decrement inventory when an order is placed. Or manually update inventory in Notion (2 minutes/day).

Q: Can I export data for accounting/taxes?

A: Yes. Use Notion exports (CSV) or integrations like Zapier to send data to accounting software (QuickBooks, FreshBooks).
