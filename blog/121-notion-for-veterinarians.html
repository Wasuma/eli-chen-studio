Core Databases for Veterinary Practice

1. Patients (The Hub)

Your central database. Each pet record includes:

  • Pet information: Name, species, breed, date of birth, weight, microchip ID
  • Owner information: Link to Client database; allows tracking multiple pets per owner
  • Medical profile: Known allergies, chronic conditions, medications, dietary restrictions
  • Vaccination status: Latest vaccines with dates; auto-flags overdue vaccines
  • Last visit: Date and reason
  • Next appointment: Date and type (wellness, surgery, etc.)

2. Clients

Owner information and preferences:

  • Owner name and contact information
  • Number of pets (linked to Patients)
  • Billing address and payment method
  • Emergency contact
  • Communication preference (email, phone, text)
  • Total lifetime value (calculate using rollups from invoices)

3. Appointments

  • Date and time
  • Patient (link to Patients)
  • Owner (auto-populated from Patient link)
  • Appointment type (wellness exam, surgery, emergency, recheck, grooming)
  • Duration estimate
  • Assigned veterinarian
  • Status (scheduled, arrived, completed, cancelled)
  • Notes (reason for visit, relevant history)

4. Medical Records & Visits

Detailed visit documentation:

  • Patient (link)
  • Date of visit
  • Reason for visit
  • Chief complaint
  • Examination findings
  • Diagnosis
  • Treatment plan
  • Medications prescribed
  • Lab results
  • Procedure notes (if surgery)
  • Follow-up recommendations
  • Next appointment suggested

5. Vaccinations & Preventive Care

  • Patient (link)
  • Vaccine name (rabies, DHPP, FeLV, heartworm, etc.)
  • Date administered
  • Next due date (auto-calculate based on vaccine type)
  • Veterinarian who administered
  • Batch number
  • Status (due, overdue, completed)

6. Billing & Invoices

  • Date of service
  • Patient (link)
  • Owner (auto-populate from Patient)
  • Service type (exam, vaccination, surgery, etc.)
  • Amount charged
  • Status (pending, invoiced, paid, overdue)
  • Payment method (cash, card, insurance)
  • Invoice number

7. Inventory

  • Item name (vaccine, medication, supply)
  • Category (vaccines, medications, surgical supplies, consumables)
  • Current stock quantity
  • Reorder level (alert when stock falls below this)
  • Unit cost
  • Supplier
  • Last reordered date

Key Views You Need

Appointment Calendar

Calendar view of Appointments, color-coded by appointment type. Drag to reschedule. Quickly see availability.

Overdue Vaccinations

Filter Vaccinations & Preventive Care view to show only Status = "Overdue". See all patients needing booster shots or annual exams.

Today's Schedule

Filtered Appointments view showing only today's appointments, sorted by time. Print or share with clinic staff.

Patient Medical Timeline

For any patient, link to Medical Records & Visits to see all past visits in chronological order.

Inventory Alerts

Filter Inventory to show items where Current Stock ≤ Reorder Level. Never run out of vaccines or critical supplies.

Monthly Revenue

Rollup in a summary page showing total revenue from Billing database, grouped by month or service type.

Practical Setup Steps

  1. Create core databases: Patients, Clients, Appointments, Medical Records, Vaccinations, Billing, Inventory
  2. Link them: Appointment → Patient → Client; Medical Record → Patient; Vaccination → Patient; Billing → Patient
  3. Set up key views: Appointment calendar, Overdue vaccinations, Today's schedule, Patient timeline
  4. Create templates: When creating a new Appointment, auto-populate patient info; when creating Medical Record, auto-link vaccination history
  5. Add formulas: Auto-calculate "next due date" for vaccines based on vaccine type; flag overdue vaccinations in red
  6. Configure access: Veterinarians have full access; techs have limited access (no salary/billing info); clients get read-only access to their pet's records

Pro Tips for Veterinary Practices

  • Color-code by species: Red for dogs, blue for cats, green for exotic. Easier scanning.
  • Create pre-op/post-op checklists: Surgical cases have higher risk; standardized checklists prevent mistakes.
  • Track medication refills: When a patient is prescribed a long-term medication, record refill due date in their Medical Record.
  • Client communication template: After each visit, auto-generate an email summarizing findings, medications, and next steps.
  • Breed-specific health alerts: Golden Retrievers prone to hip dysplasia; add a reminder field for age-appropriate screening.
  • Staff roles: Assign appointment types to veterinarians (Dr. Marcus does surgeries, Dr. Sarah does exotics).

Handling Sensitive Medical Data

Veterinary records are private. While Notion isn't formally HIPAA-certified (it's for human healthcare), these practices help:

  • Use strong passwords: Enable 2FA on your Notion workspace
  • Control access: Only veterinarians and essential staff access medical details
  • Encrypt sensitive info: Use Notion's database templates with limited visibility
  • Back up regularly: Export your Notion database monthly to a secure drive
  • Comply with state laws: Check your state's veterinary medical record retention requirements

Integration Ideas
