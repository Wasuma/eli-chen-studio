How to Organize Notion for Your Veterinary Practice

Core Databases

1. Patient Roster — Master pet database.

  • Pet name, species (dog, cat, rabbit, bird), breed, age, color/markings
  • Owner name, contact info (phone, email, emergency contact)
  • Medical history summary, allergies, medications, chronic conditions
  • Vaccination status (current, overdue, needs booster)
  • Views: "All active patients," "By species," "Overdue vaccinations," "Surgical candidates"

2. Medical Records — Visit documentation.

  • Linked to patient (auto-populate pet/owner name)
  • Visit date, presenting complaint, temperature/vitals, physical exam findings
  • Diagnosis, treatment plan, medications prescribed, dosage, duration
  • Follow-up instructions, next appointment date
  • Views: "By patient," "This week," "Follow-up pending"

3. Vaccination & Preventative Care — Health tracking.

  • Track each vaccine: rabies, DHPP (dogs), FVRCP (cats), FELV, others
  • Last administered date, expiration date, manufacturer, lot number
  • Auto-alert when vaccines are overdue (configurable reminder dates)
  • Filterable view: "All rabies overdue," "All cats needing FVRCP," "Due next month"

4. Surgical & Procedure Log — Procedure documentation.

  • Procedure type (spay, neuter, dental, biopsy, foreign body removal)
  • Pre-op assessment, anesthesia used, estimated duration, actual duration
  • Surgeon notes, intra-op complications, specimens collected
  • Post-op instructions for owner, follow-up needs, suture removal date
  • Views: "All surgeries," "Recovery schedule," "This week's procedures"

5. Appointment Scheduler — Daily schedule.

  • Date, time, patient name, owner name, procedure/reason
  • Duration, veterinarian assigned, exam room
  • Status: scheduled, arrived, no-show, completed
  • Print-ready daily schedule view

6. Medications & Prescriptions — Medication tracking.

  • Medication name, strength, form (tablet, liquid, injection)
  • Dosage, frequency, duration prescribed
  • Patient and owner info linked
  • Refill status tracking
  • Drug inventory at-a-glance

7. Invoicing & Payments — Financial tracking.

  • Create invoices from visits and procedures
  • Item-based: exam, vaccines, procedures, lab work, medications
  • Payment status (paid, pending, overdue)
  • Owner payment history
  • Views: "Unpaid invoices," "Overdue accounts," "Monthly revenue"

Typical Veterinary Workflow in Notion

Daily Workflow

  1. Start day by viewing today's appointment schedule (print or view on tablet)
  2. For each appointment, pull up patient record in medical records database
  3. After exam, update medical record with visit notes, diagnosis, treatment
  4. Prescribe medications and update vaccination status if needed
  5. Generate invoice and send to owner

Vaccination Management

  1. During exam, check patient's vaccination status in Notion
  2. If overdue, administer and update Notion with new vaccine date, lot number
  3. System automatically moves pet out of "overdue" view and into next due date
  4. Owner portal shows pet's current vaccination status

Surgical Scheduling

  1. Owner requests spay/neuter in appointment
  2. Create surgical record with pre-op assessment and surgery date
  3. Post-op instructions auto-populate in patient medical record
  4. Set reminder for suture removal follow-up
  5. Track recovery progress in follow-up appointments

Common Questions

Is this HIPAA compliant?

While HIPAA applies to human health, the AVMA has privacy standards for veterinary practices. Notion supports those standards with privacy controls and permission settings. Review Notion's security documentation and your specific practice requirements.

Can I import my current patient records?

If your current system exports to CSV or Excel, yes — you can import into Notion. This is a one-time migration effort well worth the long-term benefits.

What about multi-location practices?

Add a "Location" field to your appointment and patient databases. Create filtered views by location. Works seamlessly across locations.

How does this compare to traditional EHR software?

EHR software ($100-500/month) has more specialized features (prescription routing, lab integration). Notion ($0-10/month) gives you 80% of the functionality at 5-10% of the cost, with full customization to your practice.
